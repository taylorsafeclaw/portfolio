The final cut — 12 effects

Everything that earned its spot. Nothing decorative, nothing redundant.

What got cut and why:
Direction Aware Hover — too subtle, most users won't notice approach direction
Text Reveal Card — fights with Focus + Spotlight for attention on same element
Shimmer Border — redundant with Moving Border. Pick one.
Hover Image Links — no real images for v1, abstract patterns feel forced
Tracing Beam — scroll progress indicator on a single page is unnecessary chrome
Scroll Velocity — breathing already provides ambient life, reacting to speed is unnoticeable
Lamp Gradient — Text Generate + Erosion already handle section entrances
Split Text — competes with Text Generate. One text reveal per section.
Field Resonance — tempo shifts so subtle nobody notices. Effort not worth it.
Evervault Texture — breathing field already provides the same atmospheric role
Vanish Text — beautiful concept but you're not watching text you've scrolled past
Reveal Links — another reveal mechanic when ScrambleLink already exists
Emphasis Decode — a tempo variant of Header Decode. One decode speed is enough.
Final Dissolve — Footer Gravity already closes the page

The 12

THE IDENTITY — always on, defines the world
01 Breathing Field
tune existing

The ASCII grid at ~10% opacity with slow sinusoidal warmth waves. Characters felt, not read. The canvas everything lives on.

02 Cursor Halo
existing

Characters near cursor climb the ramp and brighten. The primary interaction with the field. Works everywhere on the page.

03 Density Sparkles
Aceternity Sparkles

Random characters flash up the ramp and settle back (·→▒→·). 2-3 per second across the viewport. Stars winking. What makes the field feel alive vs. just "a breathing background."

THE SCROLL — how content enters
04 Density Erosion
Aceternity Canvas Reveal

As each section scrolls in, ASCII characters erode outward from the content area (█→▓→▒→░→gone). Different shape per section: center-out for Story, top-down for Work, edges-in for Conviction. Content doesn't appear on the page — it emerges from the field.

05 Header Decode
MagicUI HyperText

Section headers scramble through density ramp characters on scroll-in, then resolve to readable text. Extends your existing ScrambleLink pattern to viewport triggers. The signature text interaction — fast, crisp, structural.

06 Text Generate
Aceternity Text Generate

Narrative body text materializes word by word on scroll, paced to reading speed. Each word briefly flashes through the density ramp (░→▒→letter) before resolving. The Story and Conviction sections use this. The page is being written as you read it.

THE CARDS — how work entries interact
07 Focus Cards
Aceternity Focus Cards

Hovering one work entry dims the others via density descent — non-focused cards' text drops to --fg-quietest, border characters descend the ramp. The focused card brightens. Attention is physical.

08 Card Spotlight
Aceternity Card Spotlight

Cursor creates a density gradient on the focused card's surface — a mini version of the global cursor halo, scoped to the card. Background characters climb the ramp near cursor position. Same physics, smaller scale.

09 Moving Border
Aceternity Moving Border

A density character (▓) orbits the focused card's perimeter on a 3-4 second loop. Not a glowing pixel — a character walking the border. The detail that makes someone screenshot the site.

THE CLOSE — how the page ends
10 Magnetic CTA
Aceternity Magnetic Button

The email link drifts toward your cursor on proximity. Nearby ASCII characters also drift — they're pulled in too. The invitation to reach out has physical gravity.

11 Footer Gravity
existing

Already built in scroll-store. ASCII field densifies at the bottom — characters climb the ramp as you approach the end. The page compresses to a close.

THE THREAD — between sections
12 Border Beam
MagicUI Border Beam

A density character (░) travels the hairline divider between sections, left to right. Slow, meditative. The thread connecting each thought to the next.
