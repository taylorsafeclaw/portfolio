What should happen after the intro resolves?

Three directions for the ASCII field's post-intro life

A

Fade to silence

After the wordmark resolves, the ASCII field fades out almost entirely — just the faintest texture remains. The hero becomes the wordmark + bio on a near-clean dark canvas. The ASCII had its moment in the intro; it doesn't need to keep performing.

Vibe: Confident restraint. The intro earned attention, the resting state holds it.

B

Consolidate into structure

After the intro, the random ASCII characters drift/settle into intentional patterns — forming subtle grid lines, section dividers, or density bands that become the page's structural skeleton. The noise becomes architecture.

Vibe: The chaos of creation settling into the order of a finished thing.

C

Retreat to edges

The ASCII field pulls away from center after the intro — characters migrate toward the viewport edges, forming a living border/frame around the content. Center stays clean, edges stay alive. Cursor halo still works on the edges.

Vibe: The medium is always present but never competing with the message.

These aren't mutually exclusive — B and C could combine. And all of them feed into the scroll transition question: what happens to the ASCII world as you scroll into the content sections below.
