Does this direction resonate?

Key question: should the ASCII field be the whole page surface (like Aino), or should it be a living frame/texture that content emerges from?

A

ASCII as the full surface

Like Aino — the entire page is the ASCII grid. Content is carved out of it. Navigation, section headers, everything lives in the character world. Most ambitious, most cohesive, hardest to build.

B

ASCII as living frame

The ASCII field is a persistent atmospheric layer. Content sections carve clean space out of it (erosion on scroll). The field breathes at the edges, responds to cursor everywhere, and uses density to signal meaning. Content is normal readable text — not rendered through ASCII.

C

Hybrid — ASCII-heavy hero, clean content below

The hero is fully ASCII (as it is now). As you scroll, the field erodes and thins dramatically. Content sections are clean with ASCII accents — density indicators, scramble-reveal headers, living margins. The ASCII world is most intense at the top and fades as you go deeper.
